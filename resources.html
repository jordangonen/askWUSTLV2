Resources That WashU Provides

Check out some of the links below

The First Year Center: is an excellent place to find orientation, school programs, and events. The site is a good resource for helping you find what it will be like moving in on the big day. In addition, the first year center is responsible for planning on campus housing. They oversee the arrangement of housing for freshmen and sophomores living on the South 40 as well for Juniors and Seniors living in the Village. For International students, the first year center provdies programming resources as well as special living arrangements. The lofts are often used to house international students and are located on the Delmar Loop. This is the perfect location: surrounded by a number of cultural cuisines and markets and is great for immersing yourself in the city. Lastly, the First Year Center is responsible for ensuring that students have all of their proper information filled out before coming to campus. They have lots more information on their website.

Food in St. Louis: There is somehting special about the food in St. Louis. It is one of the best parts about being in the midwest: there is a mix of culture and it is very apparent in the food. We recommend you check out trip advisor's list of the top places to eat in St. Louis. There, you can find awesome cultural options.

The Club Directory: located on the Student Union's website is one of the best resources for finding opportunities on campus. The directory lists the over three hundred clubs that we have here on campus: ranging from books and basketball to water polo to angler's club, Washington University offers a diverse mix of opportunities to get involved. There are a number of acapella groups, frisbee clubs, and even a culinary club. The site also lists contact information for how to go about getting invovled in the club, making it really easy to find your niche early on. We recommend checking out the site before the activities fair so you can get a good idea of where to be looking on Mudd Field come fall/spring.

Student Health Services: is located on the South 40. The health center is extremely conveinet for students to recieve check-ups, pick up medicine, or get immunizations. Students can access their site using their WUSTL KEY and make appointments or check on orders.

Athletic Center: is located right near the Fraternity row on main campus. The Athletic Center is currently being remodeled jsut in time for the 2016 presidenital debate. The building is expected to house multiple levels of workout/cardio equipment in addition to a full-service arena, rec center, and swimming pool.
